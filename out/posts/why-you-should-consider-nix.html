Nixos is a Linux distro built around the Nix language

About

Pros

  • Static Configuration

    Becuase the entire system is configured through 1 system, its easy to completely configure an app in a. So

  • Ephemeral Nixos

    Its that your root partition is on a tmpfs, except for the nix store and configuration dir. Since Nixos rebuilds its state from just those directories, you only need the core Nixos repos. If you are also interested in trying it, Nixos is really your only option. (to my knowledge)

  • 1 syntax format

    You can configure SystemD, Apache HTTPD, or Yggdrasil all from your nixos files.

Cons

  • Packages are somtimes slower to be upgraded

    This was my main reason for switching from Nixos to Arch in the past. However, I am no longer interested in running bleed-edge software, so its no longer an issue for me.
